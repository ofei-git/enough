Design Notes

Female-Forward, Universal Appeal
Soft backgrounds, organic color palette, warm typography. Feels like a lifestyle/wellness app, not a finance tool. The sage green reads as growth and balance rather than "money." Rounded corners, generous whitespace, no sharp edges.
Mac-Native Patterns
Sidebar navigation (accounts + categories), traffic light window controls, toolbar actions. Drag-and-drop for CSV import. Keyboard shortcuts (‚åòI for import, ‚åò1-3 for sections). Native file handling. Follows macOS Human Interface Guidelines.
Light Mode First
The warm cream backgrounds and sage accents work best in light mode. Dark mode can come later, but the primary experience should feel airy and welcoming. Most female-forward apps (Day One, Things, Bear) lead with light themes.
The Ritual Experience
Weekly review is a distinct mode‚Äînot just another tab. It has its own flow: hero summary ‚Üí quick stats ‚Üí focused categorization inbox. Progress feels satisfying (1 of 4, 2 of 4...). Completion screen celebrates the ritual, shows trajectory.
Sidebar as Navigation
Three main views: Dashboard (overview), Transactions (full list), Trends (historical). Accounts listed with balances‚Äîclicking filters to that account. Categories below for quick filtering. Collapsible on narrow windows.
Enough Number Prominence
The enough number is always visible‚Äîin the enough card ("Your enough: $85,000/year") and the progress bar. It's not hidden in settings. Every view contextualizes spending against this anchor. The monthly breakdown ($7,083) is the primary reference.
