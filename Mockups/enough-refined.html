Design Notes

Typography
Source Serif 4 for display numbers and headings—adds warmth and sophistication. SF Pro for body text. The contrast between serif display and sans-serif UI creates visual interest while maintaining readability.
Custom Iconography
Hand-picked SVG icons with 1.5px stroke weight. Each category has a distinct color-coded icon container. No emoji. Icons are simple, geometric, and feel crafted—similar to Things 3's approach.
Signature Element: Progress Ring
The circular progress indicator is a signature element—more distinctive than a bar. Shows percentage used with subtle gradient fill. Creates visual anchor for the enough meter. Inspired by Activity Rings but softer.
Depth & Layering
Multiple shadow levels create hierarchy. Cards float on canvas. Hover states lift cards slightly. The sidebar has subtle texture. Everything feels considered and intentional.
Category Selection
Full-width options instead of small chips. Each option shows icon, name, and hint. Border highlight on suggested and hover. This interaction is central to the weekly ritual—it needs to feel substantial.
The Enough Badge
Always visible in sidebar footer. Warm-toned container differentiates it from navigation. This is the anchor—the number that contextualizes everything. Persistent but not dominant.
